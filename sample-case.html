Customer Support

Scaling support operations for a global platform.

A structured approach to high-volume support—holding quality steady while volume grew.

Start a Conversation
Overview

Context about the engagement.

Our partner operated a fast-growing global platform with support demand that had outpaced their internal team. Volume was climbing across voice, chat, and email, and quality had started to vary depending on who handled each ticket.

They didn't need more headcount thrown at the problem—they needed structure. We stepped in as an extension of their team to build the processes, ownership, and quality controls that could scale with the business.

The Challenge

The problem they faced.

Response times slipped during volume spikes, and escalation paths weren't clearly defined—so complex tickets stalled while everyone waited to see who owned them.

Quality assurance happened after the fact, if at all. Without consistent processes, tone and resolution varied from agent to agent, and there was no reliable way to see where performance was drifting until customers complained.

The Solution

What we implemented.

We started by mapping their existing tools, tone guidelines, and escalation logic—then built our team structure around them rather than forcing a generic playbook onto their operation.

From there we layered in the structure that was missing: clear ticket-handling processes, defined escalation ownership, and a quality assurance framework with structured reviews and calibration sessions. Transparent reporting gave the partner real visibility into volume, resolution time, and quality trends—so issues surfaced early instead of after the damage was done.
