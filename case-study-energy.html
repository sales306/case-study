Energy & Battery Storage

Near real-time incident response for a European energy trading and battery storage provider

How aCXend helped an AI-powered energy trading platform turn a manual, delay-prone incident process into a near real-time detection-to-notification workflow.

The Problem

When every minute of downtime has a price tag.

A leading European energy technology company specializing in AI-powered energy trading and battery energy storage optimization manages a large portfolio of battery assets participating in energy markets. Its platform continuously monitors battery performance, asset availability, market participation, and operational health to maximize revenue opportunities for customers.

In energy trading, operational uptime is critical. Performance degradation, connectivity failures, dispatch interruptions, or system anomalies can quickly impact market participation and revenue generation — and delays in identifying and addressing these issues can mean missed trading opportunities, reduced grid services revenue, contractual penalties, and customer dissatisfaction.

As the company's customer base and managed asset portfolio expanded, a gap emerged. The monitoring platform could reliably detect anomalies, but the downstream process — creating service tickets, notifying customers, and coordinating a response — still relied on manual intervention. That introduced delays during critical incidents and increased the risk of inconsistent communication.

Depending on market conditions and asset commitments, a critical battery storage issue could cost $5,000 to $20,000 per affected asset within the first 15 minutes of downtime — with losses compounding the longer an issue went unresolved.
The Approach

Closing the gap between detection and action.

Leveraging its expertise in customer experience transformation, process optimization, and intelligent automation, aCXend conducted a comprehensive assessment of the client's incident management lifecycle, customer communication processes, and operational workflows — with one objective: reduce the time between anomaly detection and customer awareness to the shortest possible window.

aCXend designed and implemented an automated incident orchestration framework connecting the client's monitoring platform, ticketing system, and CRM environment. When an anomaly, performance deviation, or operational disruption was detected, automated workflows immediately created a service ticket — enriched with asset information, customer details, severity classification, and predefined response priorities — eliminating manual ticket creation and the delays that came with it.

To strengthen customer engagement, aCXend layered in a multi-channel notification strategy. Automated email notifications fired immediately upon ticket creation, giving customers:

  • The nature of the detected issue and its potential business impact
  • Affected assets and recommended next steps
  • Incident reference details for tracking and escalation

For high-severity incidents, aCXend supplemented automation with a human-led outreach process — dedicated support personnel made proactive outbound calls to confirm customer awareness, validate receipt of notifications, and accelerate remediation. This combination of automation and human intervention created a fail-safe that kept customers informed across multiple channels, significantly reducing the likelihood of missed alerts. Real-time performance monitoring and reporting on ticket volumes, response times, and escalation rates rounded out the framework, enabling continuous refinement.

The Outcome

From multi-step manual process to seconds.

By automating incident detection-to-notification workflows, the company achieved near real-time customer alerting — dramatically reducing the delay between issue identification and customer action. What previously required multiple manual steps could now be executed within seconds, letting customers respond faster to critical battery storage issues, protect market participation, and reduce exposure to financial penalties and lost revenue.
